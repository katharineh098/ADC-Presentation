Sampling Rate

how often an ADC takes samples of the continuous analog

Sampling Theorem

"To reconstruct a signal, the sampling rate must be at least twice the highest frequency component."

Quantization

the process of mapping continuous infinite values to a smaller set of decrete finite values

Quantization Error

the difference between an analog signal's actual value and its nearest representable ADC value

Encoding

the process of representing the digital output of the ADC as a binary number

Resolution

the number of distinct digital values that can be used to represent the input signal

Types of ADC

Flash ADC

Utilizing multiple comparators in parallel, fast but expensive

Can convert very high frequencies of input signals

Consume more power than other ADCs and they are limited to low resolutions, typically up to 8 bits

Successive Approximation ADC

Efficient and commonly used

Dominate the medium to high-resolution ADC market

Sampling rates of up to 25Msps and resolutions ranging from 8 to 18 bits

Sigma-Delta ADC

High resolution, used in audio applications

A closed-loop oversampling technique

Designed to sample a slowly varying input signal multiple times, a process known as oversampling.
